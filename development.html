Summary of "Tweleve Pillars" by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener (Chapter 4-7)



Chapter 4 - Achieve Your Goals is the fourth pillar. I've read and heard this advice to write down your goals numerous times. They become real when they are written. In addition to helping you achieve your goals, it also improves you as a person. The main benefit of creating goals is how motivated they make you to achieve them. Plans will alter, but your goals will remain the same, as I just learnt from someone else. "Plan, Do, Check, Adjust" is a useful method for monitoring your progress. In the book, Michael and Charlie discuss the importance of writing down goals. Michael admits feeling frustrated over the years because he desperately wanted to be successful, but hadn’t known where to start..Charlie merely advised Michael to write down his aspirations. Setting goals in writing helps them become real instead of just ideas in your head. A good quote from the book is “The major reason for setting a goal is for what it makes of you to accomplish it.”

Chapter 5 - Fifth Pillar: "The Proper Use of Time" The pain of discipline weighs only a few ounces, however the pain of regret weighs several tons. Don't put off taking action since the end of your life will arrive sooner than you think. Spend each day wisely because there is only one best opportunity each day, therefore keep in mind that every day you spend is one less day you have to spend. The best chances are ones that fit in with your long-term objectives. Understand the distinction between important and urgent matters.Days are costly and priceless! A day wasted means one less day available for other activities. Make sure you wisely invest each one. The majority of individuals focus on the urgent above the important when using their time. People fall into the time management trap all the time. If you let it, the urgent is a master who will make you his slave. Being in control of time is preferable to being the other way around. Being the architect of our lives is crucial! If you don't create your own life plan, you probably will end yourself in someone else's. And do you know what they have in store for you? Not much. There will be a master and a slave. If you let the day run you, it will. Control your time or it controls you.

Chapter 6 - The sixth pillar of Success: When Michael and Charlie meet this time, Michael tells that his relationship with Amy is doing great! His 100 life objectives are on paper. Michael is questioned by Charlie about his influences, particularly the people he hangs out with. Michael describes to Charlie each of his close buddies and how they interact. It turned out that he was surrounded by pals who didn't do much to motivate him to improve and accomplish his objectives. Charlie suggests that he begin reading and explains why it is so crucial. Charlie puts Michael to the test by demanding that he finish a book before asking him for any more success tips. "Avoid easy crowds if you want to advance. Go where there are rigorous standards and performance requirements." People have a remarkable ability to shape your future. Whether it's a good, bad, or neutral association, every relationship you have is one. Consider the following queries regarding your relationships. Is anyone around me? Why are they having such an impact on me? What are they making me read? What are they making me say? Where are they taking me? What are they making me think? What do they envision myself as? Then assess your competency with the responses.
